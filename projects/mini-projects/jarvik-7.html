Model of Jarvik 7 Total Artificial Heart (TAH)

An in-depth educational project to understand the working mechanism, components, and functionalities of the Jarvik 7 Total Artificial Heart.

Introduction to the Jarvik 7 Total Artificial Heart (TAH)

The Jarvik 7 Total Artificial Heart (TAH) is one of the most remarkable innovations in medical technology, designed as a lifesaving solution for patients with end-stage heart failure. The Jarvik 7 is a pneumatically powered, total artificial heart that replaces both ventricles of the heart. This device serves as a bridge to heart transplant for patients who cannot wait for a donor heart, providing them with a mechanical heart that can keep them alive until a suitable donor is found.

Our educational model of the Jarvik 7 Total Artificial Heart (TAH) serves as an important tool for understanding how this life-saving device operates. By simplifying the complexities of the device, we aim to provide a comprehensive overview of the essential components involved in its functioning, how they interact, and their role in ensuring the heart continues to function in the absence of a natural one. The model is a conceptual representation, crafted using basic materials that replicate the main components of the actual Jarvik 7.

Explanation of the Project

The project consists of a simple model designed to simulate the primary functionalities of the Jarvik 7 Total Artificial Heart. While this model is far from being an exact replica, it demonstrates how the key components of the TAH, such as the blood pumps, pneumatic chambers, and pressure regulation cuffs, work together to simulate heart functions.

The model is powered by a motor that mimics the pumping action of the heart, circulating fluid through tubes that simulate blood flow. The pneumatic chambers, made from thermacol, represent the ventricles of the heart, and the valves are symbolized by bottle caps, providing an analogy to the mechanical valves in the actual Jarvik 7 heart.

By creating this model, we intend to provide students, professionals, and anyone interested in medical technology with a better understanding of how this artificial heart works, its components, and the mechanisms that sustain a human being during an extended wait for a transplant. It is essential to note that this is not a working prototype of the real device but a simplified educational model to grasp its general operation and core components.

Components of the Jarvik 7 Total Artificial Heart

The Jarvik 7 Total Artificial Heart comprises various components that work in unison to mimic the functions of a natural human heart. These components are carefully designed to perform vital tasks such as pumping blood, regulating blood pressure, and ensuring proper circulation. Understanding these components is key to understanding how this life-saving device works.

Real Components vs Our Model

Real Jarvik 7 Components

  • Mechanical valves
  • Pneumatic chambers for blood flow regulation
  • Blood pumps that circulate blood
  • Cuffs to regulate pressure within the heart
  • Electrical components for heart control and function

Our Model Components

  • Valves: Represented using bottle caps.
  • Pneumatic Chambers: Simulated using thermacolmaterial to represent ventricles.
  • Blood Pump: A small motor drives the pump mechanism in our model.
  • Pressure Regulation: Simulated with adjustable plastic tubes.
  • Control System: A simple electrical circuit controls the pumping motion.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the model of the Jarvik 7 Total Artificial Heart serves as a valuable educational tool for understanding the basic principles behind artificial heart technology. Although the device itself is complex and requires specialized materials and expertise, our model simplifies these complexities, providing a clear representation of its key components and operations.

This project emphasizes the importance of innovation in healthcare technology, showcasing how engineers and scientists work tirelessly to develop life-saving solutions for people suffering from severe heart conditions. The Jarvik 7 is an extraordinary example of how mechanical engineering and medical science converge to provide hope to patients and extend their lives. Our model aims to inspire further interest in this fascinating field and encourage the development of even more sophisticated medical devices in the future.
