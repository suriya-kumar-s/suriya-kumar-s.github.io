Model of Jarvik 7 Total Artificial Heart (TAH)

An educational model to understand the working and components of the Jarvik 7 Total Artificial Heart.

This project presents a model of the Jarvik 7 Total Artificial Heart (TAH), which is a conceptual representation designed to understand the working mechanism and the various components present in the original Jarvik 7 TAH. It is not a working prototype of the actual device but serves as an educational tool to help visualize how the heart functions and the critical components involved in its operation. The Jarvik 7 heart is a pneumatically powered artificial heart, used as a bridge-to-transplant for patients suffering from end-stage heart failure.

Components of the Jarvik 7 Total Artificial Heart

Real Jarvik 7 Components vs Our Model

Real Jarvik 7 Components

  • Valves (mechanical)
  • Pneumatic chambers
  • Blood pumps
  • Cuffs for pressure regulation
  • Electrical components for control

Our Model Components

  • Valves: We used a bottle cap to represent the valves.
  • Pneumatic Chambers: Thermacol model to represent chambers.
  • Blood Pumps: A motor to simulate the pumping mechanism.
  • Cuffs for Pressure Regulation: Nebulizer parts are used to represent the cuffs.
  • Electrical Components: Tubes and other materials to replicate the graft and wiring system.

How Does It Work?

The Jarvik 7 TAH model works by simulating the actual functionality of a pneumatically powered heart. The motor is used to circulate fluids, while the bottle cap valves simulate the heart's valves for blood flow regulation. The thermacol chambers mimic the heartâ€™s ventricles, and the tubes act as grafts for fluid transfer. The nebulizer parts function as cuffs, simulating pressure control in the system.
